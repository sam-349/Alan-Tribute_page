Alan turing

1912 - 1954

A paradoxical life where scientific and social destinies collide with great violence. Alan Turing, an indirect hero of the Second World War, father of artificial intelligence, mathematical genius... and yet a victim of puritanical England because of his admission of homosexuality.

Legacy Of Alan

  • 1936
    Entscheidungsproblem
    Alan Turing's groundbreaking paper where he introduced the concept of a Turing machine, laying the foundation for theoretical computer science.
  • 1937
    Turing Machine
    The Turing machine is a theoretical model of computation, fundamental to the concept of algorithms and computation.
  • 1940
    Codebreaking at Bletchley Park
    Turing played a pivotal role in breaking the German Enigma machine codes, contributing significantly to the Allied victory in World War II.
  • 1950
    Turing Test
    Turing proposed the "Turing Test" as a measure of a machine's ability to exhibit intelligent behavior equivalent to that of a human.
  • 1952
    The ACE Computer
    Alan Turing designed the Automatic Computing Engine (ACE), one of the earliest stored-program computers, advancing computer architecture.
